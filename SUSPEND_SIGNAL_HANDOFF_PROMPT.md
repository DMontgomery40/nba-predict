## 0. The actual question (read this twice — earlier passes got the emphasis wrong)

> **Can prediction-market activity give bet365 traders an early, trader-usable "something is off with this game — review or auto-suspend the in-play props NOW" signal, when an NBA player-prop has gone bad due to a stat misallocation / mis-credit / paired-player attribution error?**

"We do not know yet" is an acceptable, possibly correct, answer — but it must be earned with real experimentation, not asserted.

### The core mental model (do not lose this)
- A stat mistake **cascades / dominoes** across the whole board for a game. A miscredited rebound moves: that player's rebound O/U, that player's triple-double / combo (PRA) markets, the **team's quarter/team-rebounds** market, **and the rightful player's** rebound + derivative markets. The single exact prop is frequently the **thinnest, lowest-volume, lowest-volatility** market — so the **loudest, earliest signal is the whole board lurching relative to its own baseline**, not the exact prop diverging. This is *why* "game-state volatility" exists in this repo; it is the **primary hypothesis**, not an afterthought.
- The desired output is **binary-ish and decision-only**: "something is for sure off here — carefully review, or (if the signal is loud enough and well-tested) auto-suspend in-play flash bets on this game." It is **explicitly NOT** "here is which prop and why and who should be credited" — that is impossible to produce in seconds and is not the goal.
- **Real people watching live** (TV, or in-arena beating broadcast delay) reprice prediction markets in real time. The hypothesis is that this human, real-time reaction sometimes carries truth **before the data providers** (NBA feed, Odds-API, bet365's model) have it.
- **The painful scenario this protects against:** the bettor was wrong, the NBA API was *also* wrong, both wrong → the book pays out against the truth. The NBA sometimes **corrects the stat days later** (the league can be — not even "allegedly" anymore — corrupt/unreliable). A live tripwire could let traders suspend before taking adverse action on a state that is about to be revealed as false.

### The label insight (this is what makes a real backtest possible)
- A **post-hoc correction — even one made a day or two later — is hindsight ground truth**. If we *know* a prop was misallocated and later corrected, we can go **back to the board state at the exact moment of the disputed event** and ask: what did all the volatility metrics look like *then*? That converts anecdotes into a supervised problem: label = "this game had a known misallocation at time T0"; features = board-state metrics in a window around T0; target = does the board signal fire early enough and rarely enough on non-incident games.
