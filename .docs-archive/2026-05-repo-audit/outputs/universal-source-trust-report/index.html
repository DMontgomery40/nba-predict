Universal Source Trust

Which sources are right earlier, which are right later, and which are merely fast — across game markets, broad props, and player props.

Internal Bet365 trader-inspection brief · persisted live data only · All persisted live quotes, Feb 16 – May 21 2026 (NBA playoff window). Not all-time.

Executive read

Across 863 settleable NBA games in the persisted live store, this brief separates two questions a trader conflates at their peril: who is actually right (settled accuracy, where deterministic truth exists) and who is merely fast or merely present (timing, leadership, coverage). Player props are treated as the primary subject.

Read this before the tables

  • Accuracy ≠ timing ≠ coverage. A low Brier means a source's probabilities were well-calibrated against realized outcomes. It does not mean that source moved first, nor that it even quoted the market. These are scored on different axes and never blended into one "trust score".
  • Settled truth is partial. Moneyline / spread / total are settled from real final scores. Player props are settled from reconstructed play-by-play box scores for the 63/64 games where reconstruction reconciled to the final score. Everything else is timing/leadership, explicitly labelled.
  • Brier is confounded by line placement. A perfectly efficient O/U line sits near 50/50 and yields Brier ≈ 0.25 by construction; an extreme milestone line yields a much lower Brier at the same skill. Compare within a family, prefer the like-for-like head-to-head, and read calibration slope (line-mix invariant).

Game winner (moneyline)

Best pregame: Kalshi (Brier 0.171, n 964). Bet365's moneyline book is thin (n 100) and should not be ranked as "weak" — it barely quotes the market.

Spread & total

Bet365 is the coverage workhorse (spread n 2,788, total n 3,604) and well-calibrated. Best pregame Brier: spread Polymarket, total Kalshi — but on small exchange samples.

Player props — the headline

Bet365 owns coverage (only source for combos & milestones) but its prop quotes do not reprice live — pregame value ≈ final value across n 33,241 props. Kalshi & Polymarket reprice through the game. On the same player+stat+line, the three sources are within Brier ~0.005 — no skill edge; the aggregate gaps are line-mix structure.

Who moves first

Among prediction markets, Kalshi tends to lead Polymarket in repricing player props (86 vs 19 instruments). Bet365-vs-exchange lead is a toss-up and thin, because Bet365 props are pregame-only.

Validation gate: custom moneyline-pregame settlement reconciles to the repo's own getSignalQualityReport exactly across all three sources (Bet365 n104, Kalshi n972, Polymarket n446).

Why "Live / final" and "Post-final +48h" Briers are near zero (and not predictive)

A quote captured during or after a game increasingly knows the outcome, so its Brier collapses toward zero. That is convergence, not foresight. The predictive checkpoint is Pregame close; the later columns are shown only to demonstrate that every source eventually agrees with reality, and that most game-market books stop quoting well before final_at (hence the large stale-excluded counts at the later checkpoints).

Player-prop deep dive

The single most operationally important finding

Bet365's player-prop quotes in this store do not reprice live: the last quote before tip is identical to the last quote before the final whistle across 33,241 settled props (pregame Brier 0.176 = live Brier 0.176). Kalshi and Polymarket repriced through the game — their live Brier collapses to 0.048 / 0.032. For a Bet365 desk, this means the exchanges are your live early-warning radar on player stat instability; your own prop feed (via the Odds-API backup path) is effectively a pregame snapshot.

How to read this without being fooled by line placement

Bet365's combination props (PRA, PR, PA, RA) sit near Brier 0.25 / accuracy 0.50 — that is what a well-centered O/U line looks like, not poor skill. Single-stat families show higher accuracy because the lines are more extreme (a starter "over 9.5 points" is ~90% yes). Polymarket's higher aggregate Brier on points/rebounds/assists is likewise a line-mix effect; see the like-for-like comparison below, where the gap nearly vanishes.

Analytical appendix

Measured, repo-backed facts above are kept separate from the venue-structure interpretation below. The following is informed reasoning about why the measured patterns appear, drawing on the public structure of each venue. It is editorial context, not additional measurement.

Why Kalshi tends to lead Polymarket on player props

Kalshi is a CFTC-regulated exchange with a centralized order book, market-maker obligations, and contracts that resolve on official statistics. That structure rewards fast, disciplined repricing on new information and tends to produce tight, frequently-updated quotes. Polymarket is a crypto-settled CLOB whose flow skews more retail and whose liquidity in thin NBA player-prop markets is patchier; its price often follows once a move is established. The measured asymmetry (Kalshi leading on the large majority of shared props) is consistent with the exchange being the price-discovery venue and the CLOB being the confirmation venue — exactly the role the desk memo assigns them ("Kalshi for intraday velocity, Polymarket for cross-market confirmation").

Why Bet365 owns prop coverage but reprices pregame-only here

Bet365's strength is breadth: as a full-service book it prices combination props (PRA, PR, PA, RA), milestones, and double/triple-double markets that the exchanges simply do not list. That is why it is the only source with settled accuracy across the combo families. The pregame-only behaviour is an ingestion artifact, not a book limitation: the persisted Bet365 prop quotes arrive through the Odds-API backup discovery path, which captures pregame snapshots and does not stream live prop reprices. The real Bet365 trading book certainly moves live; this store just doesn't see it. The operational implication stands regardless: on this console, the exchanges are the live radar.

Why like-for-like accuracy is near-identical across venues

Efficient markets on the same contract converge to similar probabilities; the residual differences are vig, line selection, and microstructure noise rather than forecasting skill. The ~0.005 Brier spread on shared instruments is well within sampling noise for these sample sizes. The practical lesson for a trader: do not chase a venue because its aggregate prop accuracy looks better — that is a function of which lines it chooses to offer. Trust the venue that (a) covers the market you need, (b) reprices live when you need a live signal, and (c) leads rather than follows. Those are coverage, liveness, and leadership — not settled accuracy.

What a Bet365 trader should actually do with this

  • Game winner / spread / total: trust the exchange consensus for the winner; trust your own well-calibrated spread/total book for those, using the exchanges as a divergence tripwire.
  • Player props, pregame: any of the three is fine on a shared line; prefer the one that actually lists the prop (usually Bet365 for combos/milestones).
  • Player props, live: watch Kalshi first, Polymarket second. Treat a Kalshi move with no corresponding move in your own line as a suspend-and-inspect trigger — your line may be stale.
  • Structural alerts: Polymarket concentrated off-price prints are the only persisted trade-level weirdness signal today; weight them, but know they are single-venue.
